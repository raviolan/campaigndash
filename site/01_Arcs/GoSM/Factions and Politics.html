      Scarlet Brotherhood

      The Scarlet Brotherhood is a secretive faction seeking to restore the Suloise noble houses to power.

      Feywild version

      Core idea

      • Secret society of fanatics who believe:
      • Their blood/people (“Suel” in canon) are destined to rule.
      • Everyone else is a tool, shield, or stepping stone.
      • Methods:
      • Infiltration, blackmail, assassination, quiet coups.
      • Use local resentment and fear to turn towns against “outsiders”.

      In Saltmarsh region (your version)

      • Goals:
      • Destabilise the coast so they can grab power and influence.
      • Control key trade routes and magical assets (ward-stones, leaks, artifacts).
      • Tactics:
      • Embed agents in:
      • Council-adjacent roles,
      • Smuggling rings,
      • Road officials (e.g., checkpoint Aroom brother),
      • Taverns like the Bent Willow Tavern.
      • Stir up:
      • Hate for Lizardfolk & dwarves,
      • Suspicion of Radiant Citadel/foreigners,
      • “Strong leader” sentiment.

      Cyrieth Nerolien's relationship with them

      • He’s a fixer / broker they use when subtlety and deniability matter.
      • He:
      • Helps weaken old protections (ward-stones, certain rituals, key people) to make the region unstable.
      • Connects Scarlet Brotherhood money to local criminals and mercenaries, then disappears before things blow up.
      • They don’t fully trust him; he doesn’t fully trust them. But he profits from their chaos.

      Table flavor

      • Visuals: scarlet cords, red-thread charms, small red knots carved in wood.
      • Phrases: “order,” “purity,” “proper rule,” “our own kind in charge.”
      • Use them as shadowy pressure, not necessarily the Big Boss – perfect mid-tier antagonists and connective tissue.

      Saltmarsh Version

       Key NPCs:

      1. Skerrin Wavechaser (LE male human assassin):
      2. - Primary agent in Saltmarsh, manipulating Anders Solmor and the council.
      • Engineers tensions between traditionalists and loyalists to weaken the town.
      1. Scarlet Brotherhood Operatives:
      2. - Often unnamed in the original material, they could be spies or merchants placed in strategic positions.
      • Motivated by the restoration of the Suel Imperium’s power.

      Motives:

      • Destabilize Keoland and Saltmarsh.
      • Ignite war between Keoland and the Sea Princes, weakening both sides.
      • Manipulate Saltmarsh's council to install Scarlet Brotherhood agents in positions of power.

      Key Black Market Figures

      1. Captain Xendros (CE female tiefling priest):
      2. - Represents Iuz, acquiring rare items and facilitating illicit trade.
      • Maintains a black market stall in Saltmarsh, offering cursed magical items.
      1. Gellan Primewater (NE male human noble):
      2. - Wealthiest smuggler in Saltmarsh, operating under the guise of legitimate trade.
      • Uses connections to the Sea Princes and other smugglers to maintain his cartel.
      1. Aroom Brothers (Acolytes of Trade in DSNM):
      2. - Operate as intermediaries for rare and illicit goods between Radiant Citadel markets and Saltmarsh's black market.
      3. Keledek the Unspoken (LE male human mage):
      4. - A secretive figure facilitating magical smuggling, working with Gellan Primewater and Captain Xendros.
      5. Potential Cyrieth Nerolien Ties:
      6. - Cyrieth Nerolien's aliases—The Serpent, The Hunter, The Shadow Broker—hint at his influence as a rogue agent in the Radiant Citadel and Saltmarsh black markets.

      Saltmarsh Council

      The Saltmarsh Council consists of local leaders balancing traditionalist and loyalist agendas, with occasional clandestine influences.

      1. Eda Oweland (CG female human noble):
      2. - Leader of the traditionalist faction and advocate for Saltmarsh's fishing industry.
      • Suspicious of outsiders and the dwarves’ mining efforts.
      • Opposes changes brought by the crown.
      1. Gellan Primewater (NE male human noble):
      2. - Wealthy merchant with a facade of respectability but secretly runs smuggling operations.
      • Supports traditionalist goals but uses his council position to maintain his illegal ventures.
      1. Anders Solmor (LG male human noble):
      2. - Youngest council member, representing progressive views.
      • Naïve and manipulated by his butler, Skerrin Wavechaser, a Scarlet Brotherhood agent.
      • Opposes smuggling and the Sea Princes but unknowingly aids the Scarlet Brotherhood.
      1. Eliander Fireborn (LN male human gladiator):
      2. - Captain of the town guard and leader of the loyalist faction.
      • Represents the crown’s interests in Saltmarsh, advocating for law and order.
      1. Manistrad Copperlocks (LN female dwarf veteran):
      2. - Leader of the dwarven mining operation, placed on the council by royal decree.
      • Focused on the mine’s success and supports loyalist goals.

      Saltmarsh Others

      1. Skerrin Wavechaser (LE male human assassin):
      2. - Anders Solmor’s butler and a Scarlet Brotherhood agent.
      • Uses his influence to destabilize Saltmarsh and further Brotherhood goals.
      1. Keledek the Unspoken (LE male human mage):
      2. - Town wizard with secret ties to smugglers and Gellan Primewater.
      • His arcane knowledge is a potential asset to the council’s political players.
      1. Hanna Rist (NG female human commoner):
      2. - Owner of the Snapping Line inn and informally supports traditionalists by hosting community events.
      3. Wellgar Brinehanded (CG male human priest):
      4. - Cleric of Procan and a respected religious leader who can sway public opinion.

      Radiant Citadel Governing

      The Speakers for the Ancestors govern the Radiant Citadel, representing its connected cultures and ensuring the balance of laws and traditions.

      1. Siabsungkoh Representative (adaptable):
      2. - Could be linked to the Aroom brothers or another merchant figure, influencing Saltmarsh trade.
      • Focused on cultural preservation and commerce.
      1. Court of Whispers Delegates:
      2. - Collect and disseminate information from across the Radiant Citadel network.
      • Black market operators or informants could subtly influence decisions without formal membership.
      1. Key Speaker (Optional for Plot Expansion):

      6. - A Speaker connected to Tihildur’s mother’s elven heritage could complicate the council dynamics, linking Saltmarsh to Radiant Citadel politics. 

      Radiant Citadel Others

      1. Court of Whispers Agents:
      2. - Operate through covert channels, trading information that could influence council decisions in Saltmarsh or Radiant Citadel politics.
      3. Potential Scarlet Brotherhood Infiltrators:
      4. - Could exist within the Citadel’s bureaucracy, manipulating its governing structures to align with Brotherhood goals.
