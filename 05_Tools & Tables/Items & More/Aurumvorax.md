## Official D&D Lore
The aurumvorax, also known as the "golden gorger," is a small but incredibly dangerous creature from classic D&D lore. Originally from earlier editions, it appears in "Journey Through the Radiant Citadel" for 5e. These creatures are:

- **Size**: Small beast, measuring around 3 feet long, 18 inches tall, weighing ~500 pounds
- **Appearance**: Eight-legged, shaggy, badger-like creatures with golden fur
- **Diet**: Known for eating gold and precious metals (hence "golden gorger")
- **Habitat**: Burrow-dwelling creatures that live in holes and underground lairs
- **Behavior**: Territorial and aggressive, especially protective of hoarded treasure

**Key Abilities** (from community 5e conversions):
- Natural Armor AC 21
- Multiattack with bite and claw attacks
- Keen Smell for advantage on Perception
- Metal Eater ability to bypass metal-based resistances
- Pounce attack to knock targets prone

## True History
- Originally the [[Dawn Incarnate]] spirit of [[Saltmarsh]] region
- Took form of peaceful aurumvorax - a sacred, protective guardian
- Connected to [[Radiant Citadel]] through [[Emerald Heart]] artifact
- Tied to [[Lizardfolk]] spirituality and their role as Speakers of the Ancestors

## Corrupted Propaganda
- **Public Memory**: Monstrous predator that devours precious metals
- **Dwarf Stories**: Cautionary tales for greedy prospectors  
- **Current Reputation**: Extinct for generations, or mythical creature
- **Fear Factor**: Unnamed forces distorted reputation to prevent restoration

## The Nightmare Aurumvorax
- **Creator**: [[Kedjou]] working with [[Sahuagin]]
- **Purpose**: Reinforce fear and prevent [[Dawn Incarnate]] restoration
- **Nature**: Twisted, corrupted version of the original guardian
- **Goal**: Keep portal to [[Radiant Citadel]] permanently sealed

## Political Implications
- **[[Sahuagin]] Agenda**: Prevent powerful outsiders from challenging their coastal dominance
- **[[Lizardfolk]] Memory**: Remember true aurumvorax nature as protector
- **[[Kedjou]]'s Ambition**: Use as testbed for his own dark magical experiments
- **Restoration Risk**: Could reopen gateway to [[Radiant Citadel]] and invite intervention
